From it's humble origins in the B.02 lab, the company has grown from strength to strength and is now one of the leaders of cloud services in Ireland and abroad.

Software as a Service

Salesforce is a well-established supplier of Software as a Service (SaaS) solutions. Their main focus is CRM (Customer Relations Management) software, with their goal being to provide a platform for effective CRM management, in the cloud, which can be used by SMEs as well as Multinational companies. Essentially, SaaS means that the software package sold is actually located in the cloud, and for a fee you are allowed access to it. Microsoft has recently shifted their business model with Office365 to a similar system.

Platform as a Service

A great example of a Platform as a Service (PaaS) is also one of my personal favourites, the Cloud9 IDE. Traditionally IDEs (Integrated Development Environments) were desktop applications, but due to advancements in cloud technology a service such as Cloud9 was created, allowing developers to work freely, anytime, anywhere, on any device. What a Platform as a Service does is it allows developers to create and run their work in the cloud, without having to set up seperate applications or services for programming and deployment.

Infrastructure as a Service

Amazon Web Services is the dominant player in the Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) market. It offers a complete server infrastructure and cloud computing services to customers of all sizes. Even popular companies such as Netflix rely on AWS infrastructure to power their services. What Infrastructure as a Service offers is physical server space and bandwidth, along with firewalls, all from one convenient cloud service. This eliminates the need for local servers for many companies, which can become costly.

Communication as a Service

Communication as a Service (CaaS) are essentially any services that enable businesses to communicate. These services may include VoIP (Voice over IP) software such as Skype, and various email providers. Many of these email services are free, a prime example of this being Gmail, one of the most popular cloud-based email clients in the world. After being established in 2004 by Google, it quickly grew to become the most well-known and used email client, bypassing Yahoo and AOL who were dominant at the time.
